Triassic

The Triassic period is right after the Permian extinction, the biggest extinction that killed off 90% of all life on earth. The pelycosaurs, the ruling species during the Permian, evolved during the Triassic to become the Archosaurs and eventually Dinosaurs. Archosaurs ruled during most of the Triassic. It wasn't until the early Jurassic did the dinosaurs rule supreme. The Triassic extinction is a part of the top 5 most brutal extinctions.

Jurassic


The Jurassic period brought forth an opportunity for the Dinosaurs to take the throne. The archosaurs weren't able to adapt to the after - Triassic ecosystem so they died off and left the throne open to dinosaurs. The Jurassic brought forth some of the more notable and famous of the Dinosaurs; Dilophosaurus, Stegosaurus, and Brachiosaurus.



Dinosaurs:

Stegosaurus

Cretaceous


The cretaceous period brought forth some of the most famous of dinosaurs; Tyrannosaurus Rex, velociraptor, Triceratops. This era was the longest era last of the three. It lasted until the extinction event famous for killing off the dinosaurs 65 million years ago. This event gave rise to mammals and eventually humans.
